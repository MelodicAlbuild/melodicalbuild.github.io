# Circuit Seasons System

## Concept

Each Circuit zone cycles through three **seasons** as the player progresses. Seasons control what content is available, how NPCs behave, what the evil team is doing, and when gyms can be challenged. This gives the player flexibility in *how* they complete each Circuit while providing natural story pacing.

---

## The Three Seasons

### 1. Offseason
**When:** Player first arrives in a zone (or revisits before triggering Circuit Season).

**What's happening:**
- The zone is peaceful. People are going about daily life.
- Gyms are **closed** for challenges — leaders are training, traveling, or doing community work.
- You can explore freely: catch Pokemon, find items, meet NPCs, discover the zone's lore.
- The Nexus subsidiary is operating quietly — you notice small signs of their activity (trucks on routes, workers in restricted areas, NPCs complaining about environmental changes).
- Your rival may show up casually — friendly battle, hints about what they've heard.
- **Side content is available:** side quests, NPC fetch quests, hidden areas, optional dungeons.

**Player goal:** Explore, prepare, build your team for the upcoming Circuit.

### 2. Circuit Season
**When:** Triggered by a story event (varies per circuit — see triggers below).

**What's happening:**
- The Circuit is officially open. Banners go up, crowds arrive, the zone buzzes with energy.
- All 4 gyms in the zone **open simultaneously** — player can challenge them in any order.
- Gym leaders scale based on how many badges you've earned **in this circuit** (0-3), so the difficulty adapts to your chosen path.
- Routes have more trainers (Circuit challengers from across Erova).
- The Nexus subsidiary **ramps up operations** — they're exploiting the Circuit crowds as cover. Workers in restricted areas become guards. You start encountering Nexus Grunts.
- Key story beats happen: confrontations with Nexus Admins, rescuing Pokemon, discovering research notes.
- Your rival is also competing — you'll battle them mid-Circuit and they may beat some gyms before you.

**Player goal:** Earn 4 badges, uncover the Nexus plot in this zone.

### 3. Festival Season
**When:** Triggered after earning all 4 badges in the circuit AND completing the Mini League.

**What's happening:**
- The zone celebrates the end of the Circuit. Streets are decorated, special vendors appear.
- **Festival-exclusive content:** rare item shops, unique battles, mini-games, cosmetic rewards.
- The Nexus plot in this zone reaches its climax — their operation is exposed, and you must stop them.
- The **legendary dungeon** for this zone opens (the environmental damage from Nexus awakens/disturbs the legendary).
- After resolving the legendary encounter, the path to the next zone opens.
- NPCs have unique Festival dialogue — gratitude, celebration, hints about the next zone.
- Special Pokemon encounters (attracted by the festival energy, rare spawns).

**Player goal:** Enjoy the festival, stop Nexus, encounter the legendary, move on.

---

## Season Flow Per Circuit

```
[Arrive in Zone] → OFFSEASON (explore, prepare, side content)
       ↓
[Story Trigger] → CIRCUIT SEASON (4 gyms in any order, Nexus ramps up)
       ↓
[4 Badges + Mini League] → FESTIVAL SEASON (celebration, Nexus climax, legendary, path forward)
       ↓
[Resolve Zone] → Zone stays in FESTIVAL permanently (revisitable with festival shops/NPCs)
       ↓
[Travel to Next Zone] → Next zone starts in OFFSEASON
```

## Gym Leader Scaling

During Circuit Season, each gym leader has **4 team variants** based on how many badges the player has earned in that specific circuit:

| Badges Earned (This Circuit) | Gym Leader Team Level | Team Size | Difficulty |
|--------|----------------------|-----------|------------|
| 0 (first gym) | Zone base level | 2-3 Pokemon | Standard |
| 1 | Base + 3 levels | 3 Pokemon | Moderate |
| 2 | Base + 6 levels | 3-4 Pokemon | Hard |
| 3 (last gym) | Base + 9 levels | 4 Pokemon + held items | Intense |

**Example — Circuit 1 (Verdant Coast, base Lv 12):**
- If Normal gym is your 1st: Leader has Lv 12-14 team, 2 Pokemon
- If Normal gym is your 4th: Leader has Lv 21-23 team, 4 Pokemon with held items
- Water/Flying/Bug gyms scale the same way

This means:
- Every playthrough can be different
- No gym feels "too easy" or "too hard" regardless of order
- Replaying with different routes keeps things fresh
- Friends can compare strategies ("I did Bug first, it was rough early but...")

## How Seasons Affect the World

### Wild Encounters
| Season | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Offseason | Standard encounter tables. Calm, normal rates. |
| Circuit Season | Higher-level Pokemon appear (trainers attract stronger wilds). Some exclusive encounters. |
| Festival Season | Rare/special Pokemon appear (drawn by energy/celebration). Festival-exclusive spawns. |

### NPC Behavior
| Season | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Offseason | Casual dialogue. Gym leaders found around town. Shops have normal stock. |
| Circuit Season | Excited dialogue. Gym leaders in their gyms. Extra trainers on routes. Pokemart restocks battle items. |
| Festival Season | Celebratory dialogue. Special vendors. Gym leaders attend the festival. Unique gift NPCs. |

### Nexus Corporation Activity
| Season | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Offseason | Subtle signs — NPCs mention strange activity, blocked paths, environmental clues. |
| Circuit Season | Active — Grunts on routes, Admins in restricted areas, environmental damage visible. Story confrontations. |
| Festival Season | Climax — Major base infiltration, subsidiary boss fight, legendary disturbance, resolution. |

### Music
| Season | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Offseason | Calm, ambient zone theme. |
| Circuit Season | Upbeat, energetic variant of zone theme. |
| Festival Season | Festive, celebratory variant with unique instruments. |

## Revisiting Old Zones

After completing a zone, the player can return. Zones stay in **permanent Festival state** with:
- Festival shops still open (unique items per zone)
- Higher-level wild encounters (scaled to player progress)
- Optional rematches with gym leaders at full power
- Post-story side quests that weren't available before
- NPCs reference your accomplishments

---

## Story Integration with Nexus Corp

The Seasons system ties perfectly into the villain arc:

**Circuits 1-2:** Nexus subsidiaries seem like unrelated local problems. Offseason reveals clues, Circuit Season confrontations feel like side issues, Festival exposes the subsidiary.

**Circuit 3:** Player connects the subsidiaries to Nexus Corp. The pattern becomes clear — every zone, same cycle, same corporate playbook. Finding "Project Convergence" notes.

**Circuit 4:** Nexus drops the pretense. Circuit Season includes open legendary capture attempts. Festival becomes a race to save the legendary before Nexus gets it.

**Circuit 5:** Full invasion. Offseason is tense (Nexus already occupying the zone). Circuit Season is resistance/infiltration. Festival is liberation + Director Haze reveal.

**Circuit 6:** No real Offseason — the Spire is under siege. Circuit Season is the ascent against Nexus forces. Festival is the final celebration after stopping the convergence.
